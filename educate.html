Impacts of Artificial Intelligence on Education

Living in the world of ours, where technology is so advanced, it is no doubt that many children are interested in technology. Because of that, many schools have looked into making lessons more enjoyable by incorporating technology into them. Although teachers are irreplaceable due to artificial intelligence still lacking human skills, like emotions and wise decision making, they have already made their way into education systems worldwide.

    Ways AI has impacted education

  • Automated Admin Tasks for teachers

    An educator spends a tremendous amount of time grading homework and tests. However, AI can quickly do that, while at the same time offering recommendations for how to close the gaps in learning. Although machines can already grade multiple-choice tests, they are very close to being able to assess written responses as well. This allows more time for teachers to spend with each student to coach them on thier doubts.

  • Differentiated and Individualized learning

    Adjusting learning based on an individual studentâ€™s particular needs has been a priority for educators for years, but AI will allow a level of differentiation that teachers would not be able to reach. There are several companies currently developing AI that can teach, test and feedback to students from pre-K to college level and gives them the challenges they are ready for. They identify gaps in knowledge and redirects to new topics when appropriate. As AI gets more advanced, it might be possible for a machine to analyse a student's face that indicates they are struggling to grasp a subject and will modify a lesson to respond to that.

Pros of AI
  • Chances of error is reduced when dealing with complicated data
  • AI does not wear out and get tired easily
  • We can let AI do repetitive jobs
  • AI can be used in healthcare sectors around the world
Cons of AI
  • Lack of creativity and out of box learning
  • No emotions (which can lead to bad decision making)
